# Entry & Exit Strategy Used by VCs

A self-project breaking down how venture capital firms structure investments, manage risk, and generate returns — from deal mechanics to exit strategy — anchored by a detailed case study of Byju's rise and collapse.

## Contents

- **What VC firms are and how they operate** — funding role, revenue model (management fees + carried interest), and the fundraising → sourcing → diligence → investment → value-add → monitoring → exit lifecycle
- **Investment profile** — stage focus (seed to Series B), sector priorities (tech, healthcare, fintech, consumer, sustainability), and diversification approach
- **Deal mechanics** — liquidation preference, anti-dilution/ratchet clauses, blocking and voting rights, drag-along/tag-along rights
- **Risk hedging** — syndication, lead/follower co-investment structures
- **Probability of success** — why compounding failure probabilities across functions (management, product, demand, pricing) crush startup survival odds, and how the power law shapes VC returns
- **Stages of VC funding** — Pre-Seed through Mezzanine/Pre-IPO, capital use and investor expectations at each stage
- **Key evaluation metrics** — Revenue Growth Rate, Gross Margin, CAC, CLTV, IRR, and portfolio diversity, each with formulas and worked examples
- **Case Study: Byju's** — timeline from 2011 founding to $22B peak valuation (2022) to ~80% valuation collapse (2023–24), unit economics breakdown (revenue growth vs. loss growth, EBITDA margin, subscriber churn), and takeaways on governance and "growth at all costs" risk
